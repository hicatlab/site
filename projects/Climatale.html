CoWeave —
co-creative
narrative with AI

An interactive storytelling system that lets writers and AI collaboratively author branching narratives in real time — preserving your voice while opening up new creative possibilities.

The Problem

Generative AI writing tools often feel like autocomplete on steroids — they complete sentences, but they don't collaborate. Writers frequently report a loss of agency: the AI overtakes their narrative voice, dilutes their style, and produces text that is fluent but hollow.

Existing co-writing systems treat the human as a prompt engineer and the model as the author. CoWeave inverts this: you remain the primary author, and the AI acts as a responsive collaborator — offering branches and alternatives without overwriting your intent.

"The best co-author isn't one who writes for you — it's one who opens doors you didn't know existed."

— Exit interview, Participant 12

Our Approach

CoWeave introduces a branching narrative canvas where every authored segment can spawn parallel AI-generated continuations. Writers navigate this tree, cherry-picking phrases, recombining branches, or using AI text purely as scaffolding to rewrite entirely.

Under the hood, we fine-tune a compact language model on author-provided samples to capture stylistic fingerprints, conditioning generation on a learned voice embedding — extracted from as few as 500 words of the writer's own prose.

1
Upload a writing sample. CoWeave extracts a voice embedding from 500+ words of your prose, capturing rhythm, vocabulary, and stylistic tendencies.
2
Write freely on the canvas. Every segment you author becomes an anchor point. The system watches your prose — never interrupting.
3
Explore AI branches. At any point, request parallel continuations. They sound like you — because they were conditioned on your voice embedding.
4
Keep, discard, or remix. Cherry-pick a phrase, recombine two branches, or use the AI text as a provocation to write something entirely your own.

Key Findings

In a user study with 24 fiction writers — ranging from hobbyists to published authors — participants using CoWeave produced 38% longer narratives in the same time window versus a baseline LLM chat interface, and rated their sense of authorial ownership significantly higher (p < .01).

Qualitatively, writers described CoWeave as feeling like a "brainstorming partner" rather than a ghostwriter. Several participants reported using AI branches not as text to keep, but as provocations — springboards that helped them discover directions they then wrote entirely themselves.

What's Next

We're extending CoWeave to support multi-author collaboration — where multiple human writers and multiple AI personas co-author a single narrative, each with distinct voice embeddings. We're also exploring educational creative writing tools and applications in game narrative design.

We welcome collaborators from HCI, NLP, and the digital humanities. Reach out if this work resonates.
